Overview

The Brief

All Things Design's annual showcase is the organization's highest-visibility moment of the year — a student-run design event attended by peers, faculty, and industry guests. The print campaign needed to establish a theme that felt creatively ambitious while remaining producible on a student organization budget.

As External Brand Director, I was responsible for the full campaign art direction: concepting the theme, directing a team of three designers, and personally designing the poster series and event program. Everything was produced for risograph printing to keep costs low and quality high.

Role & Process

How I Approached It

I led a team of three designers across a six-week sprint, balancing creative direction with hands-on design work.

01

Theme Development

Ran a two-hour ideation session with the core team to generate campaign concepts. We narrowed to three territories and pressure-tested each against the event's tone, budget, and printing constraints before landing on the final direction.

02

Art Direction

Created a visual brief covering the chosen theme, color palette (constrained to two spot colors for riso), typographic approach, and compositional principles. Shared with the team to align before any design work began.

03

Poster Series Design

Designed a series of five posters in varying sizes — A2, A3, and A4 — that could function independently or as a cohesive wall installation. Each poster featured a different speaker or program element.

04

Production & Print Supervision

Prepared all files for risograph output, managed press checks, and oversaw final production. Delivered 200 units of the event program and 50 posters across three sizes.
