Overview

The Brief

Verdant Co. had been operating for six years under a visual identity built at launch — a wordmark that no longer reflected the maturity and ambition of the brand. They needed a full rebrand to position themselves for enterprise clients while retaining the warmth that made them successful with smaller studios.

The challenge was balance: too polished and they risk alienating their existing community; too casual and they lose credibility in the room with larger clients. I was brought in to lead visual identity from strategy through final delivery.

Role & Process

How I Approached It

I led this project end-to-end over eight weeks, from initial discovery through brand guidelines handoff.

01

Brand Audit & Discovery

Interviewed five key stakeholders and reviewed two years of marketing materials. Ran a competitor audit across 12 brands in the same space to identify whitespace in the category.

02

Positioning & Moodboarding

Synthesized audit findings into three distinct positioning territories, each with its own visual language. Presented moodboards to align the team before any logo work began.

03

Identity Design

Developed two logo directions, each tested across a range of lockups, sizes, and backgrounds. Refined the chosen direction through three rounds of client feedback.

04

System & Handoff

Built a complete brand system including typography scale, color tokens, iconography guidelines, and a 40-page brand guide. Delivered Figma source files and print-ready assets.
